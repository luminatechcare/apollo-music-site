Studio Story

About Apollo Music Company

A studio where classical tradition, contemporary creativity, and patient teaching come together.

Our Story

Rooted in musicianship, shaped by students

Apollo Music Company was founded on a simple belief: every person is capable of making meaningful music when they’re given the right tools, time, and encouragement. What began as a small studio has grown into a dedicated space for students of all ages to explore piano, guitar, and collaborative music-making.

Lessons are intentionally unhurried and relational. We balance clear structure with room for curiosity, so students can build solid technique while still feeling the joy that drew them to music in the first place.

Whether you’re preparing for auditions, returning to music after a long break, or starting fresh, our goal is the same: to help you feel confident, expressive, and at home at your instrument.

Instructor

Meet your teacher

Your instructor is a classically trained musician with formal degrees in performance and over 15 years of teaching experience. That background informs everything from technique and repertoire selection to how lessons are structured week to week.

Students benefit from a teacher who understands both the rigor of conservatory training and the realities of everyday life. Not every student wants to become a concert artist—and that’s okay. We meet you where you are, whether that’s preparing for juries and recitals or simply playing the music you love with more ease.

Above all, the studio is built on patience, clarity, and respect. Questions are welcome, mistakes are expected, and progress is celebrated at every stage.

Teaching Philosophy

How we teach

🎼

Musical Literacy

Students learn to read, hear, and understand music—not just memorize pieces.

🧱

Solid Technique

Healthy posture, hand position, and movement are prioritized from the beginning.

🎧

Listening First

Ear training and musical awareness are woven into lessons at every level.

✨

Joy & Ownership

Students help choose repertoire and goals, building ownership of their musical journey.
